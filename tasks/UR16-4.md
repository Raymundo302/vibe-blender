# UR16-4 — Textured emission: the emit shader's COLOR socket drives its light

## Ray's report (2026-07-12, regression + missing feature)
Image planes (emit shader) now render as plain WHITE emissive — the image is
gone. Expected model (his words, the TV-in-a-dark-room):
1. Camera ray hits an emit surface → shows the COLOR SOCKET's pixel exactly
   (image planes = screens). Strength default 1 = exact pixels.
2. strength > 1 → the surface becomes a LIGHT whose emission is per-pixel
   colored by the socket (image/gradient/value) — a glowing screen tints the
   room like a TV, not white.

## First: reproduce + root-cause
Add an emit image plane (createImagePlane mode 'emit' with a colorful test
image), render in Rendered-live, Raytraced-GPU viewport, and F12 CPU. Find
where the white comes from — likely: `shaderOverrides('emit')` maps to
emissive=[1,1,1]/shadeless without routing the color socket's IMAGE into the
emission radiance; and/or the UR10-2 emitter NEE uses the flat `emissive`
field only. Also check the UR16-1 migration for emit planes (shadeless →
emit): did it stop setting texKind/texDataUrl into the fields the shadeless
raster path reads? Report the exact break before fixing.

## The model (implement in ALL renderers)
- Emit radiance at a surface point = eval(color socket at that point) ×
  emissiveStrength, with **strength default 1 for the emit shader** (a fresh
  emit material or migrated image plane shows exact pixels; Ray's image
  planes must render their image again — pixel-equivalent to pre-UR16).
- Camera/bounce ray hit: return that radiance directly (shadeless semantics
  at strength 1 — no BRDF, matches the old shadeless look; alpha socket still
  applies).
- **NEE mesh lights (strength > 0 emitters)**: the sampled point's radiance
  uses the SOCKET EVAL AT THAT POINT — CPU: uv/local-pos lookup at the
  sampled triangle point (the emitter CDF machinery exists; weight the CDF by
  mean texture luminance × strength if cheap, else keep area-weighting and
  document); GPU kernel: same texture/gradient fetch in the light-sample
  path; Rendered-live viewport: emissive surfaces already just output their
  color — route the socket eval (it has the texture bound); no real GI there,
  glare sells it (documented since UR10-2).
- strength scales continuously — 1 = screen, 10 = strong colored glow.

## Acceptance criteria
- tsc clean; units green (+ radiance-at-point unit test with a 2-color test
  image: sampled left half emits red, right half blue).
- e2e (extend image-plane/ur10-2 or new): (1) REGRESSION: emit image plane
  renders its image again in Rendered-live AND Raytraced-GPU AND F12-CPU
  (pixel probes vs the known test PNG — the pre-UR16 image-plane checks
  exist, make them pass again); (2) TV test: dark room, emit plane with a
  half-red/half-blue image at strength 8 → floor left of the plane tinted
  red-dominant, right tinted blue-dominant, in F12 CPU AND GPU (probe both
  sides); (3) strength 1 → plane visible, room ~unlit (radiance too low to
  read above noise — loose probe); (4) gradient-driven emit also works (one
  probe); (5) gpu-parity emissive-room + gradient-alpha scenes still pass.
- Screenshot: the TV scene, LOOKED at (it should look like a TV in a dark
  room — that's the acceptance bar).
- Free-text report, FIRST LINE STATUS:. No idle-waiting on long runs.
